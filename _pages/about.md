 I am **Zijing Zhuang** [^1]. You are encouraged to call me by my birth name if you are fluent in Mandarin Chinese. Otherwise, I go by **Carole**.
 
[^1]: 庄梓敬, as written in simplified Chinese.

I am a Fifth-year Ph.D. student in Math at **Washington University in St. Louis** and on job market in academic year 2026-2027. My academic advisor is [Martha Precup](https://www.math.wustl.edu/~precup/).
 
 My academic interests are in the intersection of algebraic geometry, representation theory, and combinatorics. Specifically, I am interested in the various phenomenons raised by the studies of the flag variety. One of my main projects is about nilpotent Hessenberg varieties. I also enjoy solving math problems using coding with Mathemcatica, Mcaulay 2, and SageMath.

In my free time, I like reading, playing classical guitar, and doing martial arts.
